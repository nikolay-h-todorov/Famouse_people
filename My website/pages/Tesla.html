About Tesla

A Serbian-American inventor electrical engineer, and futurist who made significant contributions to the development of modern electrical systems. Best known with the design of the modern alternating current X-ray technology, wireless technology, robotics, electric motors and wireless electricity current theory.

Early Years

Nikola Tesla was born in 1856 in Smiljan, Croatia. As a child, he showed an aptitude for mathematics and a fascination with electricity. After studying engineering and physics in Europe, he immigrated to the United States in the 1880s and began working for Thomas Edison. However, Tesla eventually became disillusioned with Edison's approach to electricity and struck out on his own. Over the next several years, he made numerous groundbreaking contributions to the field, including the development of the alternating current motor and transformer, and the invention of the Tesla coil.
